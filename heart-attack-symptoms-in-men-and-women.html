Heart Attack Symptoms In Men And Women.

Health Care / By Dhruthi Health Care

Chest pain was once believed to be a common sign of heart attacks, but years of research have shown that this is not always the case. In actuality, there are several variables that affect symptoms, such as gender, age, and health status. This will enable you to recognize the many heart attack symptoms and help you decide whether you or a loved one needs to seek medical attention.
Here are the signs of an early heart attack:
It’s crucial to pay attention to any early signs because a lot of cardiac damage occurs in the first two hours after a heart attack. For a heart attack, it is best to get assistance as quickly as possible.
Early heart attack symptoms may appear in 50% of all heart attack victims, according to the Society of Cardiovascular Patient Care.
The following are some possible early heart attack signs:
mild pain or discomfort in your chest that may come and go, which is also called “stuttering” chest pain

  • Shoulder pain
  • Neck or jaw pain
  • Sweating
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Lightheadedness or fainting
  • Breathlessness
  • Severe anxiety or confusion
    The signs and symptoms of a heart attack might differ from person to person and even from one attack to the next. Believing in oneself is crucial. You are the expert on your body.
    Symptoms of Heart Attack in Men:
    In the general population, men experience heart attacks at a rate that is over two times higher than that of women. In comparison to women, men also get heart attacks sooner in life. Your risk of suffering a heart attack is increased if you smoke cigarettes, have a history of high blood pressure, or high cholesterol, are obese, or have other risk factors.
    Male heart attack symptoms include:
  • Standard chest pain/pressure that feels like “an elephant” is sitting on your chest, along with severe upper body pain or discomfort in the arms, left shoulder, back, neck, jaw, or stomach. The chest pain may come and go or be persistent.
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Stomach discomfort that feels like indigestion
  • Shortness of breath, which may leave you feeling like you can’t get enough air, even when you’re resting
  • Dizziness or feeling like you’re going to pass out
  • Breaking out in a cold sweat symptoms of a heart attack in women scientists have recently come to the conclusion that women can experience heart attack symptoms quite differently from men.
    While chest pain and tightness are still the most typical symptoms in women, many often-reported symptoms are very different from those that are typical in men. Women typically wait longer than men to seek medical attention if they believe they are suffering a heart attack, which may be due in part to a lack of information about the differences in symptoms between the sexes.
    Symptoms of a heart attack in women include:
  • Unusual fatigue lasting for several days or sudden severe fatigue
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Anxiety
  • Lightheadedness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Indigestion or gas-like pain
  • Upper back, shoulder, or throat pain
  • Jaw pain or pain that spreads up to your jaw
  • Pressure or pain in the center of your chest, which may spread to your arm base your decision to seek care on what feels normal and abnormal for you. If you are experiencing symptoms that feel new to you, and don’t agree with your doctor’s conclusion, get a second opinion.
